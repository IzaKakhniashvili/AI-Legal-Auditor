1. Purpose and Scope
1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes the standards and procedures for the procurement of goods and services and the management of
vendor relationships at Meridian Holdings Group ("the Company"). The policy ensures that procurement activities are
conducted in a manner that is transparent, competitive, ethical, and in the best interest of the Company while maintaining
compliance with applicable laws and regulations.
1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All procurement of goods, services, and works by the Company and its subsidiaries
- All departments and business units initiating or managing procurement activities
- All employees involved in the procurement process, including budget holders, requisitioners, and approvers
- All vendors, suppliers, contractors, and service providers engaged by the Company
- Both direct and indirect procurement activities
1.3 Definitions
- Vendor: Any external entity that provides goods, services, or works to the Company.
- Preferred Vendor: A vendor that has been pre-qualified and approved for specific categories of goods or services.
- Sole Source: A procurement where only one vendor is capable of providing the required goods or services.
- Purchase Order (PO): A formal document issued by the Company to a vendor, indicating the types, quantities, and
agreed prices for goods or services.
- Statement of Work (SOW): A document that defines the scope, deliverables, timeline, and terms of a service
engagement.
- Service Level Agreement (SLA): A commitment between the Company and a vendor defining the level of service
expected.

2. Procurement Principles
2.1 Value for Money
All procurement decisions must demonstrate value for money, considering not only price but also quality, reliability, total cost
of ownership, sustainability, and risk. The lowest-price option is not necessarily the best-value option.
2.2 Competition
Procurement must be conducted through competitive processes to ensure fair pricing and prevent favoritism. The following
competitive requirements apply based on contract value:
Contract Value (Annual) Minimum Requirement
Up to $10,000 Single quotation with documented justification
$10,001 - $50,000 Minimum 3 written quotations
$50,001 - $250,000 Formal Request for Proposal (RFP) with minimum 3 bidders
$250,001 - $1,000,000 Formal RFP with minimum 5 bidders and evaluation committee
Over $1,000,000 Formal RFP with minimum 5 bidders, evaluation committee, and
Board approval
2.3 Sole Source Justification
Sole source procurement is permitted only in exceptional circumstances and requires written justification approved by the
Procurement Director and CFO. Acceptable justifications include:
a) Only one vendor possesses the unique capability, expertise, or intellectual property required
b) Compatibility with existing systems or equipment that cannot be reasonably modified
c) Genuine emergency that does not permit time for competitive procurement (limited to 90 days)
d) Follow-on work where changing vendors would result in significant duplication of costs
Sole source justifications must be documented and filed with the Procurement Department. The Procurement Director must
review all sole source arrangements annually.
2.4 Ethical Conduct
All procurement activities must be conducted with the highest standards of ethical behavior:
a) No employee may participate in a procurement decision where they have a personal or financial interest
b) Confidential information received during procurement processes must not be disclosed to unauthorized parties
c) All vendors must be treated fairly and equitably throughout the procurement process
d) Employees must comply with the Company's Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy (POL-ABC-002) in all vendor
interactions

3. Vendor Qualification and Selection
3.1 Vendor Registration
All vendors must be registered in the Company's Vendor Management System before any purchase order or contract is
issued. Registration requires:
a) Complete company information (legal name, registration number, address, ownership structure)
b) Tax identification and registration documents
c) Banking information for payment purposes
d) Insurance certificates as required (see Section 3.3)
e) Compliance certifications and declarations
f) References from at least three other clients
3.2 Vendor Assessment Criteria
Vendors are assessed and scored on the following criteria:
a) Financial Stability (20%): Credit rating, financial statements, going concern assessment
b) Technical Capability (25%): Relevant experience, qualifications, certifications, infrastructure
c) Quality Management (15%): Quality certifications (ISO 9001), quality control processes, defect rates
d) Compliance (15%): Regulatory compliance, anti-bribery certifications, data protection capabilities
e) Sustainability (10%): Environmental certifications, sustainability practices, carbon footprint
f) Price Competitiveness (15%): Total cost of ownership, payment terms, volume discounts
3.3 Insurance Requirements
All vendors providing services on Company premises or handling Company assets must maintain:
a) Commercial General Liability insurance: Minimum $2,000,000 per occurrence
b) Professional Liability / Errors & Omissions: Minimum $1,000,000 per claim
c) Workers' Compensation: As required by applicable law
d) Cyber Liability insurance: Minimum $5,000,000 (for vendors handling Company data)
e) Commercial Auto Liability: Minimum $1,000,000 (where applicable)
Insurance certificates must be provided annually and must name the Company as an additional insured.

4. Contract Management
4.1 Contract Requirements
All vendor engagements exceeding $10,000 in total value must be governed by a written contract. Contracts must include:
a) Clear scope of goods or services to be provided
b) Pricing, payment terms, and invoicing requirements
c) Delivery schedules or service levels
d) Quality standards and acceptance criteria
e) Warranties and representations
f) Intellectual property rights and ownership
g) Confidentiality and data protection obligations
h) Insurance requirements
i) Indemnification provisions
j) Limitation of liability
k) Termination provisions (see Section 4.3)
l) Dispute resolution mechanisms
m) Compliance with Company policies including Anti-Bribery (POL-ABC-002) and Data Protection (POL-DP-001)
n) Right to audit
o) Force majeure provisions
4.2 Payment Terms
Standard payment terms are Net 45 days from receipt of a valid invoice. Deviations from standard payment terms require
approval as follows:
Payment Terms Approval Required
Net 30 days Procurement Manager
Net 15 days or less Procurement Director and CFO
Advance payment (any amount) CFO and General Counsel
Milestone-based payments Procurement Director
Advance payments exceeding 20% of total contract value are prohibited without Board approval. All invoices must reference a
valid purchase order number and be matched against received goods or completed services before payment.
4.3 Contract Duration and Termination
a) Initial contract terms should not exceed 3 years without approval from the Procurement Director
b) Automatic renewal clauses are permitted only with a maximum of 2 renewal periods of 1 year each and must include a
termination for convenience clause

5. Vendor Performance Management
5.1 Performance Monitoring
All vendors with contracts exceeding $50,000 annually must be subject to regular performance monitoring. Performance
reviews must be conducted:
- Monthly: For critical service providers
- Quarterly: For significant vendors ($50,000 - $500,000)
- Semi-Annually: For all other monitored vendors
5.2 Key Performance Indicators
Vendor performance must be measured against defined KPIs including:
a) Delivery Performance: On-time delivery rate (target: 95% minimum)
b) Quality Performance: Defect/error rate, compliance with specifications
c) Responsiveness: Response time to inquiries and issues
d) SLA Compliance: Achievement of agreed service levels (target: 99.5% for critical services)
e) Invoice Accuracy: Percentage of invoices requiring correction
f) Safety Performance: Incident rate for on-site vendors
5.3 Vendor Scorecard
A formal vendor scorecard must be maintained for all monitored vendors. The scorecard must be shared with the vendor
during performance review meetings. Vendors consistently scoring below 70% over two consecutive review periods must be

5.4 Vendor Escalation
Performance issues must be escalated according to the following hierarchy:
- Level 1: Operational contact at vendor -> Company's contract manager
- Level 2: Vendor account manager -> Company's department director
- Level 3: Vendor senior management -> Company's VP or Procurement Director
- Level 4: Vendor executive -> Company's CFO (contract termination consideration)
6. Vendor Risk Management
6.1 Risk Categories
Vendor risks must be assessed across the following categories:
a) Strategic Risk: Over-dependence on a single vendor, vendor market position
b) Operational Risk: Service disruption, capacity constraints, quality failures
c) Financial Risk: Vendor insolvency, pricing volatility, currency exposure
d) Compliance Risk: Regulatory violations, anti-bribery risks, data protection risks
e) Reputational Risk: Association with vendors involved in unethical practices
f) Cybersecurity Risk: Data breaches, system vulnerabilities, access management
g) Geopolitical Risk: Sanctions exposure, political instability, trade restrictions
6.2 Vendor Concentration
No single vendor should account for more than 30% of total procurement spend in any category without explicit risk
acceptance from the CFO. For critical goods and services, at least two qualified alternative vendors must be identified and
maintained as backup options.
6.3 Business Continuity
For critical vendors, Business Continuity Plans must be:
a) Requested and reviewed during vendor qualification
b) Tested jointly at least annually
c) Updated to reflect changes in vendor operations
d) Documented in the Company's own Business Continuity Plan

7. Sustainable Procurement
7.1 Environmental Considerations
The Company is committed to sustainable procurement practices. Procurement decisions must consider:
a) Environmental impact of goods and services over their lifecycle
b) Vendor environmental certifications (ISO 14001, B Corp, etc.)
c) Carbon footprint of supply chain activities
d) Use of recycled or sustainable materials
e) Energy efficiency of products and services
f) Waste reduction and circular economy principles
7.2 Social Considerations
Vendors must demonstrate commitment to:
a) Fair labor practices and human rights
b) Non-discrimination and equal opportunity
c) Safe and healthy working conditions
d) Fair wages and benefits
e) Prohibition of child labor and forced labor
7.3 Supplier Diversity
The Company encourages supplier diversity and aims to allocate at least 15% of addressable procurement spend to diverse
suppliers, including minority-owned, women-owned, veteran-owned, disability-owned, and small businesses.
8. Purchase Order Process
8.1 Requisition
All procurement requests must be initiated through a formal requisition in the Company's procurement system. The requisition
must include:
a) Description of goods or services required
b) Quantity and specifications
c) Required delivery date
d) Budget code and cost center
e) Justification for the purchase

8.2 Approval Workflow
Requisitions must be approved according to the delegation of authority matrix (Section 4.4). The procurement system will
automatically route requisitions to the appropriate approvers.
8.3 Purchase Order Issuance
Once approved, the Procurement Department will issue a purchase order to the selected vendor. No goods or services may be
ordered without a valid purchase order, except for:
a) Emergency purchases (must be regularized within 5 business days)
b) Petty cash purchases under $500
c) Recurring utility and telecommunications charges covered by existing contracts
9. Compliance and Enforcement
9.1 Policy Violations
Violations of this policy include but are not limited to:
a) Splitting purchases to avoid approval thresholds
b) Engaging vendors without proper registration and vetting
c) Issuing or accepting goods/services without a valid purchase order
d) Participating in procurement decisions with undisclosed conflicts of interest
e) Sharing confidential bid information with unauthorized parties
f) Accepting gifts or hospitality from vendors in violation of POL-ABC-002
9.2 Consequences
Violations may result in:
a) Disciplinary action up to and including termination
b) Financial liability for unauthorized commitments
c) Reporting to law enforcement where criminal conduct is suspected
d) Vendor suspension or debarment
9.3 Policy Review
This policy is reviewed annually by the CFO and Procurement Director. Changes must be approved by the CFO and
communicated to all relevant stakeholders.
