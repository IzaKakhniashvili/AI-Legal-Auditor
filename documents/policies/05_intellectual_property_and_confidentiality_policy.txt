1. Purpose and Scope
1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes the framework for the protection, management, and enforcement of intellectual property (IP) rights and
confidential information belonging to or managed by Meridian Holdings Group ("the Company"). It ensures that all IP created
during the course of business is properly identified, protected, and leveraged for the Company's benefit while respecting the IP
rights of third parties.
1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and interns
- All forms of intellectual property including patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, know-how, and proprietary
information
- All confidential business information regardless of format (digital, physical, verbal)
- All third-party IP that the Company licenses, accesses, or uses
2. Intellectual Property Ownership
2.1 Company-Owned IP
All intellectual property created by employees during the course of their employment or using Company resources is the
exclusive property of the Company. This includes:
a) Inventions, discoveries, and improvements, whether or not patentable
b) Software code, algorithms, and technical designs
c) Literary works, reports, presentations, and documentation

2.2 Assignment of Rights
All employees and contractors must execute an Intellectual Property Assignment Agreement as a condition of engagement.
This agreement assigns to the Company all rights, title, and interest in IP created:
a) During the course of employment or engagement
b) Using Company resources, facilities, or equipment
c) Relating to the Company's current or anticipated business
d) Resulting from work performed for the Company
The assignment includes all worldwide rights in all forms and media, whether now known or hereafter devised, for the full term
of protection available under applicable law.
2.3 Contractor and Third-Party IP
When engaging contractors or third parties to create IP for the Company:
a) All contracts must include clear IP assignment provisions transferring ownership to the Company
b) Work-for-hire provisions must be included where applicable under law
c) Contractors must warrant that their work does not infringe third-party IP rights
d) Contractors must disclose any pre-existing IP that will be incorporated into deliverables
e) Licenses for pre-existing IP must be obtained on terms acceptable to the Company
f) The Company must receive perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free licenses for any contractor pre-existing IP
incorporated into deliverables
2.4 Open Source Software
The use of open source software must comply with the following:
a) All open source components must be approved by the Technology Department before use
b) An inventory of all open source software used in Company products must be maintained
c) License obligations (attribution, source code disclosure, copyleft requirements) must be identified and complied with
d) Open source software with copyleft licenses (GPL, AGPL) must not be incorporated into proprietary Company products
without approval from the General Counsel
e) Contributions to open source projects on behalf of the Company require approval from the CTO and General Counsel

3.3 Non-Disclosure Obligations
a) All employees must sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) as a condition of employment
b) NDAs must remain in effect for a minimum of 5 years following termination of employment or engagement
c) For trade secrets, the obligation of confidentiality continues indefinitely for as long as the information remains a trade
secret
d) External parties must execute an NDA before receiving any Company confidential information
e) Mutual NDAs with third parties must be reviewed and approved by the Legal Department
3.4 Confidentiality Exceptions
Confidential information may be disclosed without prior authorization only when:
a) Required by law, regulation, or court order (notification to Legal Department is required before disclosure)
b) Required by regulatory authorities with jurisdiction over the Company

4. Trade Secret Protection
4.1 Identification
Trade secrets include any proprietary information that derives economic value from not being generally known and is subject
to reasonable efforts to maintain its secrecy. The Company maintains a Trade Secret Register identifying all recognized trade
secrets.
4.2 Protection Measures
The following measures must be implemented to protect trade secrets:
a) Physical access controls to areas where trade secrets are developed or stored
b) Digital access controls with role-based permissions and multi-factor authentication
c) Encryption of all digital trade secret materials at rest and in transit
d) Marking of documents containing trade secrets with appropriate confidentiality legends
e) Limiting access to trade secrets to individuals with a documented need-to-know
f) Regular auditing of access logs for trade secret materials
g) Exit interviews that specifically address trade secret obligations
h) Clean room procedures when developing products that may be similar to competitor offerings
4.3 Trade Secret Incidents
Any suspected misappropriation, unauthorized disclosure, or compromise of trade secrets must be reported immediately to the
General Counsel. The Legal Department will:
a) Investigate the incident within 24 hours
b) Assess the scope and impact of the disclosure
c) Implement measures to mitigate further damage
d) Determine whether legal action is warranted
e) Report to law enforcement if criminal misappropriation is suspected

5.1 Customer Contracts
Customer contracts involving IP must address:
a) Ownership of custom developments and modifications
b) License scope, limitations, and restrictions
c) Warranties against IP infringement
d) Indemnification for IP infringement claims
e) Restrictions on reverse engineering
f) Survival of IP provisions beyond contract termination
5.2 Vendor and Supplier Contracts
All contracts with vendors or suppliers who may create, access, or handle Company IP must include:
a) Clear IP ownership provisions (default: Company owns all custom work product)
b) Confidentiality obligations consistent with this policy
c) Restrictions on use of Company IP beyond the scope of the engagement
d) Obligations to return or destroy Company IP upon termination
e) Right to audit vendor compliance with IP obligations
f) Representations that vendor deliverables do not infringe third-party IP
g) Indemnification for any IP infringement in vendor deliverables
5.3 Joint Development
Joint development arrangements with third parties must:
a) Be approved by the General Counsel and CTO before commencement
b) Include a detailed Joint Development Agreement specifying IP ownership
c) Clearly allocate ownership of foreground IP (created during the collaboration)
d) Address licensing of background IP (pre-existing IP contributed by each party)
e) Define rights to jointly-created IP, including commercialization rights
f) Include provisions for dispute resolution regarding IP ownership
6. IP Enforcement
6.1 Monitoring
The Legal Department maintains an ongoing IP monitoring program that includes:
a) Trademark watch services for registered marks

b) Patent monitoring for potential infringements
c) Internet monitoring for unauthorized use of Company IP
d) Competitor product analysis for potential IP violations
e) Domain name monitoring for cybersquatting
6.2 Enforcement Actions
Upon identification of potential IP infringement:
a) The Legal Department will assess the nature and scope of the infringement
b) A cease-and-desist letter will be issued where appropriate
c) Alternative dispute resolution will be considered before litigation
d) Litigation will be pursued when necessary to protect the Company's IP rights
e) Criminal referrals will be made in cases of willful counterfeiting or piracy
6.3 Defensive IP Strategy
The Company maintains a defensive IP strategy that includes:
a) Building and maintaining a strong patent portfolio
b) Registering trademarks in all relevant jurisdictions
c) Maintaining comprehensive copyright registrations
d) Participating in patent pools and cross-licensing arrangements where beneficial
e) Implementing freedom-to-operate analyses before launching new products
7. Employee Responsibilities
7.1 General Obligations
All employees must:
a) Protect Company IP and confidential information at all times
b) Report any suspected IP infringement or breach of confidentiality immediately
c) Comply with all marking and handling requirements for classified information
d) Not use personal devices or accounts for Company confidential information without IT approval
e) Cooperate with IP protection measures including audits and investigations
f) Not disclose Company IP in academic publications, conference presentations, or social media without prior approval from
the Legal Department

7.2 Departing Employees
Employees leaving the Company must:
a) Participate in an exit interview that addresses IP and confidentiality obligations
b) Return all Company materials, documents, and devices
c) Confirm deletion of all Company information from personal devices and accounts
d) Acknowledge ongoing confidentiality and non-disclosure obligations
e) Acknowledge any applicable non-compete or non-solicitation restrictions
8. Compliance and Enforcement
8.1 Training
All employees must complete IP and confidentiality training within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter. Training covers:
a) Types of IP and their importance to the Company
b) Classification and handling of confidential information
c) Employee obligations regarding IP and confidentiality
d) Common scenarios and decision-making guidance
e) Reporting procedures for suspected violations
8.2 Violations
Violations of this policy may result in:
a) Disciplinary action up to and including termination
b) Legal action for damages and injunctive relief
c) Criminal prosecution for theft of trade secrets
d) Personal liability for losses caused by the violation
8.3 Policy Review
This policy is reviewed annually by the General Counsel and CTO. Updates are communicated to all personnel through the
Company's policy management system.
